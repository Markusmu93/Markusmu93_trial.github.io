    Welcome to my professional website!

    I am a research scientist specializing in digital catalysis and optimization algorithms, with a strong background in chemistry and signal processing. My expertise lies in integrating machine learning into high-throughput experimental platforms to accelerate the discovery and optimization of heterogeneous catalysts.

    Since July 2024, I hold a PhD in Signal Processing from Centrale Lille Institute, where I developed an efficient, data-driven high-throughput batch experimentation procedure on a multi-reactor system for developing new heterogeneous catalysts. I also have an MSc in Chemistry from Ludwig-Maximilians-University (LMU) Munich, with research experience in computational chemistry at UCLA. My international academic journey has equipped me with a deep understanding of both machine learning and theoretical chemistry, allowing me to bridge the gap between advanced statistical computational methods and experimental catalysis.

    I was awarded a Marie Curie scholarship within the PEARL and Horizon2020 programs leading to my PhD project. My interdisciplinary and international research project was conducted at the intersection of chemistry and machine learning, within the frameworks of two laboratories:

    • CRIStAL: A unit under CNRS, Université Lille, Centrale Lille, and Inria, covering a wide range of research fields from computer science, automation, and signal processing to applied research.
    • UCCS: Originating from the merger of chemistry and crystallography labs, UCCS has evolved into a dynamic scientific environment that focuses on solid state chemistry. My role at UCCS was within the REALCAT lab, a semi-automated high-throughput platform aimed at the rapid development of new heterogeneous catalysts.

    Research interests

    My main research interests include:

    • Optimization algorithms
    • Design of experiments
    • Machine learning
    • Black-box expensive optimization problems under uncertainty
    • Bayesian optimization algorithms
    • Digital catalysis
    • High-throughput experiments

    This research is primarily application-centered, aiming to provide efficient and systematic approaches to catalyst optimization.
